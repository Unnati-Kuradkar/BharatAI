# Abstract

BharatAI is an AI-powered citizen assistance platform developed to simplify access to Indian government schemes, scholarships, tenders, and financial assistance programs. The platform analyzes a user's profile—including age, occupation, income, and state—to recommend suitable government initiatives and welfare programs.

The system integrates Artificial Intelligence, recommendation techniques, and web technologies to provide personalized scheme suggestions, eligibility analysis, intelligent search, and an interactive AI assistant. BharatAI aims to reduce the complexity of navigating multiple government portals by offering a single, user-friendly platform where citizens can easily discover benefits they may be eligible for.

Built using Python, Flask, SQLite, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, BharatAI demonstrates the practical application of AI in digital governance and public service delivery.

---

# Introduction

The Government of India offers thousands of welfare schemes, scholarships, subsidies, loans, insurance programs, and employment opportunities through various ministries and departments. Despite these initiatives, many eligible citizens remain unaware of available benefits due to fragmented information spread across multiple government websites.

Students often miss scholarship opportunities, farmers remain unaware of agricultural support programs, entrepreneurs struggle to identify business assistance schemes, and economically weaker sections find it difficult to determine their eligibility for government benefits.

BharatAI addresses this challenge by creating a centralized intelligent platform that recommends government schemes based on user profiles. Instead of manually searching multiple portals, users simply provide basic information such as age, occupation, income, and state, and the platform generates personalized recommendations.

The project demonstrates how Artificial Intelligence and intelligent recommendation systems can improve accessibility to government services while promoting digital inclusion.

# Methodology

The BharatAI platform follows a structured workflow for generating personalized recommendations.

## 1. User Profile Collection

Users provide basic information including:

* Name
* Age
* State
* Occupation
* Annual Income

These details form the basis for recommendation generation.

## 2. Profile Validation

The submitted information is validated before being stored in the SQLite database to ensure consistency and reliability.

## 3. Database Storage

User profiles are securely stored for future retrieval and personalized recommendations.

## 4. Recommendation Engine

The recommendation module evaluates the user's profile and compares it against predefined eligibility criteria for various government schemes.

Factors considered include:

* Age
* Occupation
* Income
* State

The system then generates personalized scheme recommendations.

## 5. AI Assistant

The integrated assistant responds to user queries related to government schemes, scholarships, loans, and welfare programs by providing relevant guidance and official resource links.

## 6. Search System

Users can search for government schemes, scholarships, and tenders using keywords, allowing quick access to relevant opportunities.

## 7. Dashboard

The dashboard displays user information alongside recommended schemes, creating a personalized citizen experience.

# Results and Discussion

BharatAI successfully demonstrates how Artificial Intelligence can simplify access to government welfare programs through personalized recommendations and intelligent assistance.

The recommendation engine effectively analyzes user profiles to suggest suitable schemes based on predefined eligibility criteria. The dashboard provides users with a centralized view of their profile and recommended opportunities, while the AI assistant offers quick responses to common queries regarding scholarships, loans, and government initiatives.

The search functionality further improves accessibility by allowing users to locate schemes using keywords, reducing the effort required to navigate multiple government websites.

Overall, BharatAI highlights the potential of AI-driven recommendation systems in enhancing digital governance and improving citizen access to welfare programs.

---

# Limitations

* Current AI assistant uses rule-based responses.
* Recommendations depend on predefined eligibility rules.
* Limited number of government schemes in the database.
* No user authentication system.
* Internet access is required for official government links.
* Live government data integration is not currently available.
